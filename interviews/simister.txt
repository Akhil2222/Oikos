Alison Simister
2002
October 24, 2025
Ms. Simister is a Westwood High School history and economics teacher. She was a student from 1998 to 2002, and interested in theater, debate, and social studies. Ms. Simister noted some memorable events at Westwood: The Mock Election of 2000, where students campaigned as candidates in the U.S. Presidential election Bush, Nader, and Gore; and the foreign languages club soccer world cup
There were a lot of people that were kind of politically activist oriented, and wanted to change the world. And I think that I don’t see as much. You know, they wanted to do community service and be active in the world, and it didn’t seem as much just for, like NHS, you got your service hours, this was more like, they were sort of radical, and wanted to teach the world.
Westwood has shifted both socially and politically, with school wide events, clubs, and activities aimed at changing the world around them—although, most of the academic culture remained.
